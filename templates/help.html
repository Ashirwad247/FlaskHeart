About Our Project

Welcome to the Heart Disease Prediction System! This project uses advanced machine learning algorithms to assess the risk of heart disease.

Age

Age is a significant factor in assessing the risk of heart disease. As individuals age, the likelihood of cardiovascular conditions increases due to the natural weakening of the heart and blood vessels.

Gender

Gender is a biological factor affecting heart disease risk. Generally, males have a higher risk of heart disease at a younger age compared to females, but the risk increases for women post-menopause.

Chest Pain Type (cp)

Chest pain type (cp) refers to the type of pain experienced, which can indicate the severity of heart-related conditions. Types range from typical angina to non-specific pain.

Resting Blood Pressure (trestbps)

Resting blood pressure is the pressure in the arteries when the heart is at rest. High blood pressure (hypertension) is a critical risk factor for heart disease and must be managed to reduce cardiovascular risk.

Cholesterol (chol)

Cholesterol levels indicate the amount of fat in the blood. High cholesterol can lead to plaque buildup in arteries, increasing the risk of heart disease and stroke.

Fasting Blood Sugar (fbs)

Fasting blood sugar (fbs) measures blood glucose levels after fasting. High fasting blood sugar levels are associated with diabetes, which is a known risk factor for heart disease.

Resting Electrocardiographic Results (restecg)

This feature describes the results of a resting electrocardiogram (ECG), which records the electrical activity of the heart. Abnormal ECG results may indicate heart problems.

Maximum Heart Rate Achieved (thalach)

The maximum heart rate achieved during exercise is used to evaluate cardiovascular fitness and heart health. Lower than expected heart rates may indicate heart problems.

Exercise Induced Angina (exang)

Exercise-induced angina (exang) refers to chest pain caused by physical exertion, indicating reduced blood flow to the heart. It is a critical indicator in heart disease risk assessment.

Oldpeak

Oldpeak measures ST depression during exercise relative to rest. It reflects how well the heart adapts to exercise-induced stress, with higher values indicating potential heart problems.

Slope

Slope refers to the slope of the peak exercise ST segment. It provides information about the heart's response to stress, with an upward slope being normal and other slopes indicating potential issues.

Number of Major Vessels (ca)

The number of major vessels colored by fluoroscopy indicates the extent of coronary artery disease. Fewer vessels suggest more severe blockage, increasing the risk of heart disease.

Thalassemia (thal)

Thalassemia refers to blood disorders that can cause anemia, which may increase the risk of heart disease if left untreated. Its effects on the heart vary with the severity of the condition.
